# Skill: Scientific Synthesis

Use when:
- A task asks for a structured hypothesis, mechanism, experiment proposal, or research synthesis.

Procedure:
1. Retrieve grounded claims, evidence, contradictions, and open questions before drafting.
2. Separate observation, inference, uncertainty, and proposed tests.
3. Express hypotheses as structured objects with provenance, predictions, minimal tests, and falsifying observations.
4. Store new hypotheses as proposed memory mutations first; do not directly insert them into the fact graph.

Quality bar:
- Grounded in cited claims or paper/project notes.
- Mechanism is explicit enough to generate a prediction.
- Minimal test and falsifying observation are concrete.
- Contradictions and risks are visible, not hidden in prose.
