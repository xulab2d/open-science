# Synthesis: Evidence Hierarchy For Correlated States

Purpose:
- Compact note on how different measurements contribute different kinds of evidence for correlated and topological states.

## Core point

No single probe carries the whole burden of interpretation. Transport, compressibility, magnetic imaging, and optical measurements answer different questions, and the strongest papers now combine them rather than pretending one modality is sufficient in every regime.

## What each modality is best at

- Transport:
  best for quantization, dissipation, and edge-transport phenomenology
- Compressibility and thermodynamics:
  best for showing incompressibility and bulk phase structure
- Magnetic imaging, RMCD, and MOKE:
  best for orbital magnetization, ferromagnetism, hysteresis, and local magnetic texture
- PL, trion spectroscopy, and pump-probe optics:
  best for filling-sensitive optical response, hidden states, and in some cases fractional quasiparticle information

## Field-level lesson

The strongest modern moiré MoTe2 papers are persuasive because they cross-check. Transport alone can miss bulk ambiguities. Optical data alone can be overinterpreted. Magnetism alone can miss whether the state is topological or merely ordered. The literature is moving toward combined evidence streams, and this workspace should reason the same way.

## Working implication

When interpreting a request, the question should be:
- what does this probe establish cleanly
- what remains ambiguous
- what orthogonal probe would most efficiently remove that ambiguity

That is usually a better framing than asking whether the current data alone are enough to declare a full state identification.
