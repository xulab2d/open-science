## Optical probes are not all asking the same question

RMCD and MOKE are closest to magnetic order and orbital magnetization. PL is often sensitive to charge configuration, excitonic environment, and magnetic proximity, and in moiré MoTe2 can also become sensitive to topological state occupancy. Raman is valuable when the real question is not static order but magnetic excitations, phonons, symmetry, or mode splitting.

That distinction matters because it is easy to talk as if every optical signal is a proxy for the same hidden variable. It is not.

## Magnetic order, proximity, and optical response

The older CrI3 and related literature established several habits that still matter:
- gate field can switch competing magnetic states
- stacking and structure can control the sign or strength of interlayer magnetism
- optical spectroscopy can reveal magnetic proximity and valley splitting
- optics can sometimes control magnetism, not just report on it

This older literature is still worth keeping near at hand because it supplies the cleanest vocabulary for hysteresis, switching, structural sensitivity, and proximity effects.

## Why the newer MoTe2 optical papers matter

The newer moiré MoTe2 literature pushes optical work into a different regime. PL and transient optical spectroscopy are no longer only probes of excitons or gaps; they can track fractional fillings, hidden states, and even quasiparticle charge. That is a deeper role than in most earlier 2D-magnet or TMDC optical work.

This is probably the most important field-level update for this workspace: optical measurements in these devices can carry directly topological information if interpreted carefully.

Anchor papers:
- Cai 2023
- Zeng 2023
- Wang 2025
- Li 2026

## Working stance

When building context for a request:
- ask what physical quantity the measurement is actually closest to
- use older 2D-magnet papers for control-knob and optical-language intuition
- use moiré MoTe2 papers for filling-specific and topology-specific interpretation
- avoid collapsing PL, RMCD, MOKE, Raman, and transport into a single generic evidence class
