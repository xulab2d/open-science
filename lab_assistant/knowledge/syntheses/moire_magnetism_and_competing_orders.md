# Synthesis: Moire Magnetism And Competing Orders

Purpose:
- Compact note on how magnetism shows up across the moiré systems most relevant to this lab.

## Core point

Moiré magnetism in these devices is not a single ferromagnetic story. The field now supports a broader view in which ferromagnetism, antiferromagnetism, orbital magnetization, and time-reversal-symmetric topological phases can all be nearby in energy. The practical consequence is that a weak or missing magnetic signal does not automatically mean “nothing interesting is there.”

## What changed

The first flagship MoTe2 papers emphasized integer and fractional anomalous Hall behavior together with ferromagnetic signatures. That established the basic zero-field Chern-insulator story. But newer work made the landscape more complex:
- fractional QSH states show that not all important correlated phases are ferromagnetic
- direct magnetic imaging showed local disorder and inhomogeneity matter at the scale of the moiré state itself
- second-band papers showed that magnetic order survives outside the first canonical filling regime
- the 2026 competing-ground-state paper made explicit that antiferromagnetism can directly compete with FCI order

## Working implication

The useful mental model is phase competition, not phase confirmation. When reading data, it is often better to ask:
- which state seems to be winning here
- what nearby state might explain the missing or distorted canonical signature
- whether the chosen probe is actually sensitive to the dominant order parameter

That is a better starting point than assuming the device either reproduces the original FQAH papers or has failed.
