# Synthesis: Xu Group Optical Probe And Control Ladder

Purpose:
- Compact note on how Xu-group optical work progressed from valley optics to correlated-state readout and then to active control.

## Core point

The important Xu-group optical trajectory is not just "more PL papers over time." It is a stepwise change in what optics is being asked to do:
- first identify valley and exciton structure
- then track charge transfer and interlayer excitons
- then read out correlated and topological many-body states
- and now actively control those states

That progression matters because current lab optical data should be interpreted with the newest rung in mind, not only with the older exciton-only literature.

## Ladder of capability

- 2012-2016 valley and exciton foundation:
  optics established spin-valley locking, valley coherence, trion control, and long-lived interlayer excitons
- 2017-2019 moire-exciton bridge:
  moire potentials became an optical design element rather than a small perturbation
- 2023 correlated-state readout:
  Cai 2023 made trion PL and RMCD part of the main evidence stream for integer and fractional Chern states
- 2024-2025 richer optical interpretation:
  trion polarization identified a zero-field composite Fermi liquid and ultrafast probes exposed hidden fractional states and their melting scales
- 2026 active control:
  optical pumping is no longer just diagnostic; it can train, switch, and pattern integer and fractional Chern ferromagnets

## Working implication

For this workspace, the default question for a new optical signature should be:
- is this a passive spectral marker
- a filling-sensitive many-body probe
- or evidence that light is modifying the state itself

That framing is usually more useful than asking only which exciton peak moved.
