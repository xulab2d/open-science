# Papers: Transport And Thermodynamic Signatures

Purpose:
- Working paper shelf for transport, compressibility, and magnetic-imaging papers that define how correlated and topological states are established experimentally.

## Transport-first landmark papers

1. Park et al., "Observation of fractionally quantized anomalous Hall effect" (Nature 2023)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06536-0
- result to remember: direct integer and fractional anomalous Hall plateaus in twisted bilayer MoTe2
- use for: strongest transport language around quantization and vanishing longitudinal resistance

2. Xu et al., "Observation of Integer and Fractional Quantum Anomalous Hall Effects in Twisted Bilayer MoTe2" (PRX 2023)
- link: https://journals.aps.org/prx/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevX.13.031037
- result to remember: independent confirmation of IQAH and near-quantized FQAH with clear electric-field sensitivity
- use for: reproducibility and phase-transition language

3. Kang et al., "Evidence of the fractional quantum spin Hall effect in moire MoTe2" (Nature 2024)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07214-5
- result to remember: transport also establishes time-reversal-symmetric fractional topology in this platform
- use for: situations where Hall-style intuition alone is incomplete

## Thermodynamic and local probes

4. Zeng et al., "Thermodynamic evidence of fractional Chern insulator in moire MoTe2" (Nature 2023)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06452-3
- result to remember: local compressibility plus magneto-optics gives bulk evidence for integer and fractional Chern states
- use for: arguments that need more than transport

5. Redekop et al., "Direct magnetic imaging of fractional Chern insulators in twisted MoTe2" (Nature 2024)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08153-x
- result to remember: magnetic imaging resolves local orbital magnetization and disorder in FCI states
- use for: local inhomogeneity, disorder, or real-space phase structure

## Higher-band and competing-state transport

6. Xu et al., "Interplay between topology and correlations in the second moiré band of twisted bilayer MoTe2" (Nature Physics 2025)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-025-02803-1
- result to remember: the second moiré band has its own correlated magnetic and topological transport phenomenology
- use for: anomalies away from the canonical first-band regime

7. An et al., "Observation of ferromagnetic phase in the second moiré band of twisted MoTe2" (Nature Communications 2025)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-59691-5
- result to remember: second-band ferromagnetism is independently observed and controlled by density and displacement field
- use for: cross-checking higher-band magnetic claims

8. "Evidence of competing ground states between fractional Chern insulator and antiferromagnetism in moire MoTe2" (Nature Communications 2026)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-71479-9
- result to remember: transport and field response can reflect direct competition between FCI and antiferromagnetic ground states
- use for: interpreting non-canonical or suppressed transport signatures
