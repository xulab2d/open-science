# Papers: Moire tMoTe2

Purpose:
- Working paper shelf for twisted or moiré MoTe2, centered on results likely to matter for current Xu Lab work.

## Foundations

1. Wu, Lovorn, Tutuc, Martin, MacDonald, "Topological Insulators in Twisted Transition Metal Dichalcogenide Homobilayers" (PRL 2019)
- link: https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.122.086402
- result to remember: twisted TMD homobilayers can host topological moiré valence bands with nonzero valley Chern number
- use for: why twisted MoTe2 is expected to support topological flat bands at all

2. Devakul, Crepel, Zhang, Fu, "Magic in twisted transition metal dichalcogenide bilayers" (Nature Communications 2021)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-021-27042-9
- result to remember: a magic-angle regime can produce very flat topological bands and interaction-driven QAH or FQAH tendencies
- use for: magic-angle and flat-band/topology language

## Gate control and first correlated magnetic phases

3. Anderson et al., "Programming correlated magnetic states with gate-controlled moiré geometry" (Science 2023)
- link: https://doi.org/10.1126/science.adg4268
- result to remember: gate field can reprogram the effective many-body lattice geometry and switch between ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic correlated regimes
- specific detail: near one hole per moiré cell, the correlated ferromagnetic insulator reaches about `14 K`
- use for: displacement field as Hamiltonian control, not just density tuning

## Integer and fractional Chern physics

4. Cai et al., "Signatures of fractional quantum anomalous Hall states in twisted MoTe2" (Nature 2023)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06289-w
- result to remember: transport, trion PL, and RMCD together support zero-field FQAH states in twisted MoTe2
- specific detail: strong states near `nu = -1, -2/3, -3/5`
- use for: optical plus magnetic sensing of topological states

5. Park et al., "Observation of fractionally quantized anomalous Hall effect" (Nature 2023)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06536-0
- result to remember: direct Hall-plateau evidence for IQAH and FQAH in twisted bilayer MoTe2
- specific detail: plateaus near `h/e^2`, `3h/2e^2`, and `5h/3e^2`
- use for: strongest direct-transport phrasing

6. Xu et al., "Observation of Integer and Fractional Quantum Anomalous Hall Effects in Twisted Bilayer MoTe2" (PRX 2023)
- link: https://journals.aps.org/prx/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevX.13.031037
- result to remember: independent transport confirmation of IQAH and near-quantized FQAH, with electric-field-driven transitions
- use for: independent confirmation and device-to-device robustness

7. Zeng et al., "Thermodynamic evidence of fractional Chern insulator in moire MoTe2" (Nature 2023)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06452-3
- result to remember: compressibility plus magneto-optics gives bulk evidence for integer and fractional Chern insulators
- specific detail: incompressible, time-reversal-breaking states near `nu = 1` and `2/3`
- use for: thermodynamic or bulk arguments

## Beyond first discovery

8. Kang et al., "Evidence of the fractional quantum spin Hall effect in moire MoTe2" (Nature 2024)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07214-5
- result to remember: twisted MoTe2 also accesses fractional QSH physics, not just ferromagnetic FQAH
- specific detail: at `nu = 3`, each edge contributes `3e^2/2h`
- use for: time-reversal-related fractional topology

9. Redekop et al., "Direct magnetic imaging of fractional Chern insulators in twisted MoTe2" (Nature 2024)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08153-x
- result to remember: local magnetic imaging directly resolves orbital-magnetization signatures of FCI states
- specific detail: robust local gaps up to about `7 meV` for `nu = -2/3`
- use for: what a magnetic imaging or RMCD-like probe can add beyond transport

10. Wang et al., "Hidden states and dynamics of fractional fillings in twisted MoTe2 bilayers" (Nature 2025)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08954-8
- result to remember: ultrafast optical spectroscopy reveals many hidden fractional states invisible in static transport
- specific detail: nearly 20 hidden states and two melting timescales, about `2-4 ps` and `180-270 ps`
- use for: optical nonequilibrium or hidden-state interpretation

## Higher bands and microscopic structure

11. Xu et al., "Interplay between topology and correlations in the second moiré band of twisted bilayer MoTe2" (Nature Physics 2025)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-025-02803-1
- result to remember: the second moiré band hosts its own ferromagnetism, field-driven Chern behavior, and unusual magnetotransport
- specific detail: around `2.2-2.7` holes per moiré cell, the paper reports large out-of-plane negative magnetoresistance and approximate `1/T` scaling
- use for: higher-band anomalies

12. Thompson et al., "Microscopic signatures of topology in twisted MoTe2" (Nature Physics 2025)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-025-02877-x
- result to remember: STM/STS resolves local signatures of topological wavefunction texture
- use for: microscopic intuition about why the bands are topological

13. An et al., "Observation of ferromagnetic phase in the second moiré band of twisted MoTe2" (Nature Communications 2025)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-59691-5
- result to remember: independent evidence for second-band ferromagnetism tuned by density and displacement field
- specific detail: Curie temperature near `3.5 K` around filling `2.58`
- use for: confirming second-band ferromagnetism is not a one-off result

## Frontier optical and competing-state papers

14. Chen et al., "Robust non-Abelian even-denominator fractional Chern insulator in twisted bilayer MoTe2" (Nature Communications 2025)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-57326-3
- result to remember: even-denominator and non-Abelian FCI scenarios are already a live theoretical target in this platform
- use for: frontier/topological-order speculation

15. Li et al., "Signatures of fractional charges via anyon-trions in twisted MoTe2" (Nature 2026)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10101-w
- result to remember: photoluminescence can access fractional charge directly through anyon-trion peaks
- specific detail: the measured binding-energy ratio tracks the expected `e/3 : e/5` charge ratio
- use for: strongest optical argument that PL can access more than phase labels

16. "Evidence of competing ground states between fractional Chern insulator and antiferromagnetism in moire MoTe2" (Nature Communications 2026)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-71479-9
- result to remember: antiferromagnetic and FCI ground states can compete in intermediate-angle devices, with electric field switching between regimes
- use for: interpreting weak zero-field magnetic signals or strong displacement-field sensitivity
