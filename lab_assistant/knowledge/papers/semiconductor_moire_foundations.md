# Papers: Semiconductor Moire Foundations

Purpose:
- Working shelf for foundational and review papers that anchor the semiconductor moire field beyond the 2023-2026 twisted MoTe2 rush.

## Early theoretical foundations

1. Wu, Lovorn, MacDonald, "Hubbard Model Physics in Transition Metal Dichalcogenide Moiré Bands" (PRL 2018)
- link: https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.121.026402
- result to remember: TMD moire heterobilayers can realize isolated flat bands described by generalized triangular-lattice Hubbard models
- use for: the earliest clean statement that semiconductor moire systems are tunable Hubbard-model platforms rather than only optical curiosities

2. Zhai, Yao, "Theory of tunable flux lattices in the homobilayer moiré of twisted and uniformly strained transition metal dichalcogenides" (Physical Review Materials 2020)
- link: https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevMaterials.4.094002
- result to remember: twisted or strained TMD homobilayers can realize tunable moire flux lattices and engineer band topology through layer-pseudospin texture
- use for: topology and gauge-field control in twisted TMD homobilayers before the experimental FQAH era

3. Xu, Guo, Xian, "Moiré flat bands in twisted 2D hexagonal vdW materials" (2D Materials 2022)
- link: https://pure.mpg.de/pubman/item/item_3357110_2/component/file_3357111/Xu_2022_2D_Mater._9_014005.pdf
- result to remember: flat-band and topological tendencies extend across a broader family of twisted hexagonal van der Waals materials, with TMDs among the most promising non-graphene platforms
- use for: broad materials-design review and why the field should not be compressed into graphene-only intuition

## Experimental correlated-state foundations before twisted MoTe2

4. Tang et al., "Simulation of Hubbard model physics in WSe2/WS2 moiré superlattices" (Nature 2020)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-020-2085-3
- result to remember: WSe2/WS2 moire heterobilayers experimentally realize tunable Hubbard-model physics with doping-controlled correlated insulating behavior
- use for: experimental foundation that semiconducting moire systems can host controllable correlation physics before the MoTe2 Chern-insulator breakthroughs

5. Regan et al., "Mott and generalized Wigner crystal states in WSe2/WS2 moiré superlattices" (Nature 2020)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-020-2092-4
- result to remember: optical spectroscopy resolved both integer Mott and fractional generalized Wigner-crystal states in a semiconductor moire lattice
- use for: strongest early evidence that optical probes can directly see correlated lattice physics in moire semiconductors

6. Shimazaki et al., "Strongly correlated electrons and hybrid excitons in a moiré heterostructure" (Nature 2020)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-020-2191-2
- result to remember: moire heterostructures can host strongly correlated electrons and hybrid excitons in the same device, with optical spectroscopy remaining sensitive to the electronic state
- use for: the bridge between correlated electrons and excitonic readout in semiconductor moire platforms

7. Wang et al., "Correlated electronic phases in twisted bilayer transition metal dichalcogenides" (Nature Materials 2020)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-020-0708-6
- result to remember: twisted bilayer WSe2 showed that twisted TMD homobilayers can host tunable correlated insulating states without relying on graphene-style magic-angle fine tuning
- use for: the experimental homobilayer foundation behind later twisted MoTe2 work

## Reviews and field-level syntheses

8. Wilson, Yao, Shan, Xu, "Excitons and emergent quantum phenomena in stacked 2D semiconductors" (Nature 2021)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-03979-1
- result to remember: stacked TMD semiconductors unify moire excitons, interlayer excitons, correlated electron states, and hybrid optical-many-body phenomena in one materials framework
- use for: broad review of why excitons and correlated states should be interpreted together in stacked semiconductors

9. Huang et al., "Excitons in semiconductor moiré superlattices" (Nature Nanotechnology 2022)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-021-01068-y
- result to remember: moire excitons are a controlled optical platform whose energies, dynamics, and diffusion are tunable by twist angle, stacking, and external fields
- use for: canonical review of moire-exciton phenomenology and optical control knobs

10. Mak, Shan, "Semiconductor moiré materials" (Nature Nanotechnology 2022)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-022-01165-6
- result to remember: semiconductor moire materials should be viewed as a unified field spanning excitons, Hubbard-model physics, magnetism, topology, and designer many-body states
- use for: the best compact field review when orienting someone new to modern TMD moire physics

## Pre-2023 topology and optical-correlation bridge papers

11. Li et al., "Spontaneous fractional Chern insulators in transition metal dichalcogenide moiré superlattices" (Physical Review Research 2021)
- link: https://journals.aps.org/prresearch/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevResearch.3.L032070
- result to remember: realistic TMD moire models already supported spontaneous fractional Chern insulating phases before the twisted MoTe2 transport discoveries
- use for: pre-experimental theoretical foundation that FCI physics in semiconductor moire bands was not a surprise after 2023

12. Jin et al., "Strong interaction between interlayer excitons and correlated electrons in WSe2/WS2 moiré superlattice" (Nature Communications 2021)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-021-23732-6
- result to remember: interlayer excitons can couple strongly to correlated moire electrons, making optical spectra a direct probe of the electronic many-body state
- use for: why optical readout is not merely adjacent to correlated-electron physics in semiconductor moire platforms

13. Pan et al., "Moiré flat Chern bands and correlated quantum anomalous Hall states generated by spin-orbit couplings in twisted homobilayer MoS2" (Physical Review Research 2022)
- link: https://journals.aps.org/prresearch/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevResearch.4.L012032
- result to remember: spin-orbit coupling in twisted TMD homobilayers can generate flat Chern bands and correlated QAH tendencies outside the specific MoTe2 parameter set
- use for: broader topological-Chern-band design logic across twisted TMD homobilayers

14. Wang et al., "Excitonic insulator in a heterojunction moiré superlattice" (Nature Physics 2022)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-022-01703-y
- result to remember: a semiconductor moire heterostructure can host an excitonic insulator, showing that moire optical platforms support interaction-driven collective states beyond simple trapped excitons
- use for: broadening the optical-many-body landscape beyond Mott/Wigner and FQAH language

15. Xu et al., "Correlated interlayer exciton insulator in heterostructures of monolayer WSe2 and moiré WS2/WSe2" (Nature Physics 2022)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-022-01702-z
- result to remember: correlated interlayer exciton insulating behavior can emerge in a moire-coupled semiconductor heterostructure and be resolved optically
- use for: foundational context for correlated excitonic phases as part of the same measurement ecosystem as moire electron phases

## Structural reconstruction and twisted-WSe2 bridge papers

16. Naik, Jain, "Ultraflatbands and shear solitons in moiré patterns of twisted bilayer transition metal dichalcogenides" (PRL 2018)
- link: https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevLett.121.266401
- result to remember: small-angle twisted TMD bilayers naturally develop ultraflat valence bands together with shear-soliton reconstruction, without relying on a single graphene-like magic angle
- use for: earliest structural-flat-band bridge from twist mechanics to strong-correlation relevance in TMD bilayers

17. Zhang et al., "Flat bands in twisted bilayer transition metal dichalcogenides" (Nature Physics 2020)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-020-0958-x
- result to remember: STM/STS directly observed localized flat bands in twisted bilayer WSe2 near both 0 degrees and 60 degrees, experimentally validating the flat-band platform in TMD homobilayers
- use for: direct experimental confirmation that twisted WSe2 really hosts localized flat bands rather than only theoretical expectations

18. Weston et al., "Atomic reconstruction in twisted bilayers of transition metal dichalcogenides" (Nature Nanotechnology 2020)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-020-0682-9
- result to remember: small-angle twisted TMD bilayers reconstruct into stacking domains and dislocation networks, so real devices are domain-structured rather than rigid moire lattices
- use for: why lattice relaxation and reconstruction must enter any serious microscopic model of twisted TMDs

19. Pan, Wu, Das Sarma, "Band topology, Hubbard model, Heisenberg model, and Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction in twisted bilayer WSe2" (Physical Review Research 2020)
- link: https://journals.aps.org/prresearch/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevResearch.2.033087
- result to remember: an applied layer potential difference can tune band topology, Mott gaps, spin-orbit terms, and effective Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interactions in twisted bilayer WSe2
- use for: direct pre-2023 theory link between displacement field and correlated or topological phase control in twisted TMD homobilayers

20. Ghiotto et al., "Quantum criticality in twisted transition metal dichalcogenides" (Nature 2021)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-03815-6
- result to remember: twisted WSe2 exhibits continuous density- and bandwidth-controlled metal-insulator transitions with a quantum-critical fan and strange-metal transport
- use for: experimental evidence that twisted TMD homobilayers access nontrivial Hubbard-model criticality beyond simple correlated insulators

21. Kennes et al., "Moiré heterostructures as a condensed-matter quantum simulator" (Nature Physics 2021)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-020-01154-3
- result to remember: moire van der Waals heterostructures form a modular platform for strongly correlated, topological, and nonequilibrium quantum simulation with many complementary experimental readouts
- use for: broad review framing why semiconductor moire papers, transport papers, and optical papers belong to one coherent field

22. Shabani et al., "Deep moiré potentials in twisted transition metal dichalcogenide bilayers" (Nature Physics 2021)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-021-01174-7
- result to remember: relaxed twisted TMD bilayers can produce moire potentials above 300 meV, dominated by structural relaxation and strain rather than interlayer hopping alone
- use for: why reconstruction strongly reshapes low-energy models, confinement, and excitonic or electronic localization in twisted TMDs

23. Magorrian et al., "Multifaceted moiré superlattice physics in twisted WSe2 bilayers" (Physical Review B 2021)
- link: https://journals.aps.org/prb/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevB.104.125440
- result to remember: lattice reconstruction in twisted WSe2 bilayers generates piezoelectric, ferroelectric, and hybridization-driven moire potentials with distinct miniband consequences
- use for: richer microscopic language for twist-angle, polarization, and valley-dependent band engineering than a single scalar moire potential

24. Angeli, MacDonald, "Γ valley transition metal dichalcogenide moiré bands" (PNAS 2021)
- link: https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2021826118
- result to remember: the Gamma-valley sector of twisted TMD homobilayers can realize honeycomb- or kagome-like moire band structures and multiorbital Hubbard physics
- use for: why higher-energy valley sectors can matter and how emergent lattice geometry extends beyond triangular K-valley pictures

25. An et al., "Lattice reconstruction induced multiple ultra-flat bands in twisted bilayer WSe2" (Nature Communications 2021)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-021-25924-6
- result to remember: near 60-degree twisted WSe2 develops multiple meV-scale ultra-flat bands generated by lattice reconstruction
- use for: direct experimental link between reconstruction and an even flatter multiband landscape in twisted WSe2

26. Xu et al., "A tunable bilayer Hubbard model in twisted WSe2" (Nature Nanotechnology 2022)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-022-01180-7
- result to remember: twisted AB WSe2 realizes a field-tunable bilayer Hubbard model with excitonic, charge-transfer, magnetic, and layer-selective insulating regimes
- use for: strongest pre-superconductivity experimental bridge from electric-field control to multicomponent Hubbard physics in twisted TMD homobilayers

27. Pei et al., "Observation of Γ-valley moiré bands and emergent hexagonal lattice in twisted transition metal dichalcogenides" (Physical Review X 2022)
- link: https://journals.aps.org/prx/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevX.12.021065
- result to remember: micro-ARPES and STM directly observed Gamma-valley moire bands together with honeycomb and kagome charge textures in twisted WSe2
- use for: direct spectroscopy anchor for Gamma-valley and emergent-lattice discussions in twisted TMDs
