# Papers: Xu Group van der Waals Magnetism And Proximity

Purpose:
- Compact Xu-group shelf for the 2D-magnetism, magnetic-proximity, and spin-lattice-control papers that still shape how this lab should read magnetic optical data.

## Discovery and gate control

1. Huang et al., "Layer-dependent Ferromagnetism in a van der Waals Crystal down to the Monolayer Limit" (Nature 2017)
- link: http://www.nature.com/nature/journal/v546/n7657/full/nature22391.html
- result to remember: CrI3 remains intrinsically ferromagnetic down to the monolayer limit, establishing atomically thin van der Waals magnetism as a real experimental platform
- use for: the canonical starting point for CrI3-era magnetic optical reasoning

2. Huang et al., "Electrical Control of 2D Magnetism in Bilayer CrI3" (Nature Nanotechnology 2018)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-018-0121-3
- result to remember: a gate field can switch bilayer CrI3 between competing magnetic states rather than only perturbing a fixed order
- use for: AFM-FM switching logic and any argument where electric field changes the magnetic Hamiltonian itself

## Optical proximity and structure sensitivity

3. Seyler et al., "Valley Manipulation by Optically Tuning the Magnetic Proximity Effect in WSe2/CrI3 Heterostructures" (Nano Letters 2018)
- link: https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.nanolett.8b01105
- result to remember: WSe2 optical response can directly sense and tune magnetic proximity from CrI3
- use for: valley splitting, polarization, and magnetic-proximity interpretation in optical heterostructure data

4. Chen et al., "Direct observation of van der Waals stacking dependent interlayer magnetism" (Science 2019)
- link: https://science.sciencemag.org/content/366/6468/983
- result to remember: interlayer magnetic order in layered magnets depends strongly on stacking registry rather than only on composition
- use for: any case where sample history, strain, or reconstruction may be changing magnetic coupling indirectly through structure

## Dynamics, excitations, and mechanical control

5. Cenker et al., "Direct observation of 2D magnons in atomically thin CrI3" (Nature Physics 2020)
- link: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41567-020-0999-1
- result to remember: spectroscopy can resolve magnetic excitations directly, not only static order parameters
- use for: when Raman- or resonance-like structure may reflect magnons or spin excitations rather than simple intensity drift

6. Cenker et al., "Reversible strain-induced magnetic phase transition in a van der Waals magnet" (Nature Nanotechnology 2022)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-021-01052-6
- result to remember: moderate strain can reversibly drive a magnetic phase transition in a van der Waals magnet
- use for: mechanical sensitivity, device packaging effects, and any analysis where strain may be an untracked control knob
