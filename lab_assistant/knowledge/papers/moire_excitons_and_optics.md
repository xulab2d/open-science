# Papers: Moire Excitons And Optical Readout

Purpose:
- Working paper shelf for moiré-exciton and optical papers that help interpret why optical probes are so powerful in modern TMD moiré systems.

## Foundational moiré-exciton papers

1. Seyler et al., "Signatures of moiré-trapped valley excitons in MoSe2/WSe2 heterobilayers" (Nature 2019)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-0957-1
- result to remember: interlayer excitons can be trapped by the moiré potential and inherit strong valley-selective optical signatures
- use for: the basic idea that moiré structure can discretize and localize optical excitations

2. Tran et al., "Evidence for moiré excitons in van der Waals heterostructures" (Nature 2019)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-0975-z
- result to remember: multiple exciton resonances emerge from the moiré superlattice potential rather than from a single uniform interlayer exciton band
- use for: recognizing that optical spectra in moiré systems naturally contain spatially structured excitonic states

3. Jin et al., "Observation of moiré excitons in WSe2/WS2 heterostructure superlattices" (Nature 2019)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-0976-y
- result to remember: moiré excitons are directly resolved in a TMD superlattice, establishing moiré control as an optical design principle
- use for: canonical evidence that moiré potentials reorganize optical spectra

4. Alexeev et al., "Resonantly hybridized excitons in moiré superlattices in van der Waals heterostructures" (Nature 2019)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-0986-9
- result to remember: moiré superlattices can hybridize exciton states rather than only localize them
- use for: mixed or redistributed spectral weight in optical data

## Why this matters for correlated-state optics

5. Cai et al., "Signatures of fractional quantum anomalous Hall states in twisted MoTe2" (Nature 2023)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06289-w
- result to remember: trion PL can act as a filling-sensitive probe of integer and fractional topological states
- use for: the bridge between exciton/trion spectroscopy and correlated-electron physics

6. Wang et al., "Hidden states and dynamics of fractional fillings in twisted MoTe2 bilayers" (Nature 2025)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08954-8
- result to remember: pump-probe optics can reveal hidden correlated states that static transport misses
- use for: nonequilibrium or transient optical interpretation

7. Li et al., "Signatures of fractional charges via anyon-trions in twisted MoTe2" (Nature 2026)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10101-w
- result to remember: trion-like optical complexes can encode fractional charge directly
- use for: strongest claim that optical response can reach topological quasiparticle properties

## Broader modern optical direction

8. Zhang et al., "Excitons in the fractional quantum Hall effect" (Nature 2025)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08274-3
- result to remember: even outside moiré TMDs, excitonic probes can resolve strongly correlated topological fluids
- use for: broader context that optical access to fractional many-body physics is becoming a serious measurement mode, not a niche curiosity
