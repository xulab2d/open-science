# Papers: Xu Group Recent Moire States

Purpose:
- Compact Xu-group shelf for the recent moire-MoTe2 and closely adjacent papers most likely to matter for current Xu Lab project interpretation.

## 2023 breakthrough papers

1. Park et al., "Observation of Fractionally Quantized Anomalous Hall Effect" (Nature 2023)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06536-0
- result to remember: twisted bilayer MoTe2 shows direct integer and fractional anomalous Hall quantization at zero field
- use for: the strongest transport-first statement that the platform hosts IQAH and FQAH states

2. Cai et al., "Signatures of Fractional Quantum Anomalous Hall States in Twisted MoTe2 Bilayer" (Nature 2023)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06289-w
- result to remember: trion PL and RMCD can act as filling-sensitive optical probes of integer and fractional Chern states in twisted MoTe2
- specific detail: the paper highlights strong signatures near `nu = -1, -2/3, -3/5`
- use for: the main optical bridge between PL/RMCD observables and correlated topological phases

3. Anderson et al., "Programming Correlated Magnetic States via Gate Controlled Moire Geometry" (Science 2023)
- link: https://www.science.org/doi/full/10.1126/science.adg4268
- result to remember: displacement field can reprogram the effective moire geometry and switch the correlated magnetic ground state
- use for: the key argument that `D` is a Hamiltonian knob, not a secondary nuisance axis

## 2024-2025 broadening of evidence streams

4. Ji et al., "Local probe of bulk and edge states in a fractional Chern insulator" (Nature 2024)
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.07157
- result to remember: local probes can resolve both bulk and edge structure in a fractional Chern insulator rather than relying only on global transport
- use for: local-versus-bulk evidence and any discussion of edge reconstruction or spatial inhomogeneity

5. Anderson et al., "Trion Sensing of a Zero-Field Composite Fermi Liquid" (Nature 2024)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08134-0
- result to remember: trion polarization can detect a compressible zero-field composite Fermi liquid in twisted MoTe2
- use for: optical markers of compressible states, not only incompressible Chern states

6. Redekop et al., "Direct magnetic imaging of fractional Chern insulators in twisted MoTe2 with a superconducting sensor" (Nature 2024)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08153-x
- result to remember: local magnetic imaging directly resolves orbital magnetization and disorder in twisted-MoTe2 fractional Chern states
- use for: when a transport or optical anomaly might actually be a real-space inhomogeneity problem

7. Wang et al., "Hidden States and Dynamics of Fractional Fillings in tMoTe2 Moire Superlattices" (Nature 2025)
- link: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-025-08954-8
- result to remember: ultrafast optics reveals hidden fractional states and dynamical melting scales that static measurements do not expose
- specific detail: the paper reports nearly twenty hidden states and two distinct melting timescales
- use for: nonequilibrium interpretation and time-domain probes of correlated moire matter

8. Thompson et al., "Visualizing the microscopic origins of topology in twisted molybdenum ditelluride" (arXiv 2024; later accepted to Nature Physics)
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.19308
- result to remember: STM/S can image the layer-pseudospin texture tied to the topological flat bands in twisted MoTe2
- use for: microscopic intuition about where the topology comes from and how strain or reconstruction could change it

9. Li et al., "Universal Magnetic Phases in Twisted Bilayer MoTe2" (Nano Letters 2025)
- link: https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.nanolett.5c04751
- result to remember: local optical spectroscopy and nanoSQUID magnetometry map a robust magnetic phase diagram across multiple twist angles
- specific detail: the paper reports spontaneous ferromagnetism at `nu = -1` and `nu = -3` from about `2.1` to `3.7` degrees, with an extra `nu = -5` ferromagnetic state at `2.1` degrees
- use for: deciding which magnetic features look universal versus device-specific

## 2026 optical and transport frontier

10. Holtzmann et al., "Optical control of integer and fractional Chern insulators" (Nature 2026; preprint available)
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.18639
- result to remember: optical pumping is now being used as an active control knob for integer and fractional Chern states, not just as a passive probe
- use for: the next-step logic where PL helicity and optical drive can manipulate correlated topology directly

11. Park et al., "Observation of dissipationless fractional Chern insulator" (Nature Physics 2026)
- link: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41567-025-03167-2
- result to remember: at least some twisted-MoTe2 devices now reach a dissipationless fractional-Chern-insulator regime rather than only near-quantized transport
- use for: the cleaner-transport benchmark when judging whether a current device is still disorder-limited

12. Li et al., "Signatures of fractional charges via anyon-trions in twisted MoTe2" (Nature 2026)
- link: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-026-10101-w
- result to remember: optical trion-like complexes can encode fractional quasiparticle charge directly in twisted MoTe2
- specific detail: the binding-energy ratio tracks the expected `e/3 : e/5` charge ratio
- use for: the strongest current claim that PL can access more than phase labels and can touch quasiparticle properties themselves
