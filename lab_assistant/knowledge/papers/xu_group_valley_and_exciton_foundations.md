# Papers: Xu Group Valley And Exciton Foundations

Purpose:
- Compact Xu-group shelf for the valley, exciton, and interlayer-exciton papers that still anchor how this lab should think about optical readout in TMD systems.

## Valley optical selection rules and coherence

1. Xiao et al., "Coupled Spin and Valley Physics in Monolayers of MoS2 and Other Group-VI Dichalcogenides" (PRL 2012)
- link: http://depts.washington.edu/xulab/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Coupled-Spin-Valley-Physics-Xu.pdf
- result to remember: spin and valley are locked together in monolayer TMDs, which is why circular polarization can address valleys selectively in the first place
- use for: baseline logic behind valley-selective optics, polarization-resolved PL, and field-induced valley splitting

2. Jones et al., "Optical Generation of Excitonic Valley Coherence in Monolayer WSe2" (Nature Nanotechnology 2013)
- link: http://depts.washington.edu/xulab/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/nnano.2013.151.pdf
- result to remember: linearly polarized excitation can create coherent superpositions of valley excitons rather than only population imbalance
- use for: polarization logic when the question is coherence, not just valley polarization

3. Ross et al., "Electrical Control of Neutral and Charged Excitons in a Monolayer Semiconductor" (Nature Communications 2013)
- link: http://www.nature.com/ncomms/journal/v4/n2/full/ncomms2498.html
- result to remember: gate-controlled doping cleanly redistributes oscillator strength between neutral excitons and trions in a monolayer TMD
- use for: why doping-dependent PL and reflectance should be treated as primary charge-state readouts rather than a nuisance background

## Interlayer excitons and heterostructure transfer

4. Rivera et al., "Observation of Long-Lived Interlayer Excitons in Monolayer MoSe2-WSe2 Heterostructures" (Nature Communications 2015)
- link: http://www.nature.com/ncomms/2015/150224/ncomms7242/full/ncomms7242.html
- result to remember: MoSe2/WSe2 heterobilayers support long-lived interlayer excitons with spatially separated carriers
- use for: the starting point for long-lived charge-separated optical probes in stacked TMD systems

5. Schaibley et al., "Directional Interlayer Spin-Valley Transfer in 2D Heterostructures" (Nature Communications 2016)
- link: http://www.nature.com/articles/ncomms13747
- result to remember: spin-valley information can transfer directionally across a heterostructure interface instead of being trapped within one monolayer
- use for: interpreting polarization transfer and interlayer optical coupling in multilayer or heterobilayer devices

## Moire-exciton bridge

6. Yu et al., "Moire excitons: from programmable quantum emitter arrays to spin-orbit coupled artificial lattices" (Science Advances 2017 preprint era)
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.07015
- result to remember: moire potentials should be viewed as a programmable excitonic lattice, not only as a perturbation to a uniform exciton
- use for: conceptual bridge from early interlayer-exciton work to modern moire-exciton and correlated-optics interpretation
