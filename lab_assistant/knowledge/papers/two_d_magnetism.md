# Papers: 2D Magnetism And Optical Control

Purpose:
- Working paper shelf for foundational 2D-magnetism and magnetic-proximity papers that overlap with Xu Lab measurement styles.

## Discovery and baseline order

1. Huang et al., "Layer-dependent ferromagnetism in a van der Waals crystal down to the monolayer limit" (Nature 2017)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/nature22391
- result to remember: intrinsic ferromagnetism survives to the monolayer limit in CrI3, with strong layer dependence
- use for: canonical optical 2D-magnet anchor

2. Gong et al., "Discovery of intrinsic ferromagnetism in two-dimensional van der Waals crystals" (Nature 2017)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/nature22060
- result to remember: intrinsic 2D ferromagnetism is a broader material-class phenomenon, not only a CrI3 story
- use for: broader field framing

## Electrical and structural control

3. Huang et al., "Electrical control of 2D magnetism in bilayer CrI3" (Nature Nanotechnology 2018)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-018-0121-3
- result to remember: gate field can switch bilayer CrI3 between competing magnetic states
- use for: AFM-FM switching and metamagnetic gate-control logic

4. Li et al., "Pressure-controlled interlayer magnetism in atomically thin CrI3" (Nature Materials 2019)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-019-0506-1
- result to remember: stacking and interlayer structure strongly control magnetic order
- use for: any argument where local structure may be driving the magnetic phase

## Optical proximity and optical control

5. Seyler et al., "Valley Manipulation by Optically Tuning the Magnetic Proximity Effect in WSe2/CrI3 Heterostructures" (Nano Letters 2018)
- link: https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.nanolett.8b01105
- result to remember: optical spectroscopy can resolve and tune magnetic proximity effects in WSe2/CrI3
- use for: valley splitting, polarization, and magnetic-proximity interpretation

6. Zhang et al., "All-optical switching of magnetization in atomically thin CrI3" (Nature Materials 2022)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-022-01354-7
- result to remember: light can actively switch magnetic order, not just passively probe it
- use for: pump- or power-dependent magnetic changes

## Spectroscopy beyond static order

7. Cenker et al., "Direct observation of two-dimensional magnons in atomically thin CrI3" (Nature Physics 2021)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-020-0999-1
- result to remember: Raman spectroscopy can access magnetic excitations and not just the order parameter
- use for: excitation-spectrum logic

## Beyond insulating CrI3-like magnets

8. Fei et al., "Two-dimensional itinerant ferromagnetism in atomically thin Fe3GeTe2" (Nature Materials 2018)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-018-0149-7
- result to remember: 2D magnetism also includes itinerant metallic ferromagnets with different phenomenology
- use for: broadening comparisons beyond CrI3
