## Chunk 1: What this historical reservoir represents

This reservoir captures a mature earlier phase of the lab’s work:
- internal data summaries by material system
- manuscript and figure-development folders
- theory notes tied to specific experiments

It is useful because it shows:
- how the lab moves from exploratory measurements to figure logic
- which experimental signatures were important enough to anchor papers
- the style of internal scientific argument that survived publication pressure

## Chunk 2: Major material families visible in the archive

The archive is organized around several recurring systems:
- CrI3
- CrI3-WSe2 heterostructures
- CrSiTe3
- A2CrCl4
- NiPS3
- VS2
- EuS-MX2 heterostructures

Interpretation:
- the lab’s 2D-magnet history is not a single project but a family of optical and magneto-optical programs centered on magnetism, heterostructure coupling, and gate/field control

## Chunk 3: Scientific themes that recur across the archive

Recurring themes:
- magnetic proximity in van der Waals heterostructures
- spin/valley control via magnetic coupling
- gate control of magnetic phase transitions
- hysteresis and domain behavior
- spatial heterogeneity and domain-resolved signals
- optical access to magnetic order through PL, MOKE, RMCD, and related contrasts

This is the main reusable lesson:
- the lab repeatedly treats optical and magneto-optical observables as windows into magnetic state, not just as spectroscopy for its own sake

## Chunk 4: What the paper folders reveal about lab priorities

From the CrI3-WSe2 and gated-CrI3 manuscript material:
- paper logic is organized around a small number of decisive figure families
- supplementary figures are used to justify extraction methods, power dependence, band alignment, spatial maps, and domain modeling
- cover letters emphasize “first demonstration,” strong control knobs, and clear device-level functionality

What matters operationally:
- manuscript folders are excellent for recovering final figure logic and claim hierarchy
- they are less useful for quick day-to-day project-state reconstruction than the summary decks

## Chunk 5: Figure logic that survived toward publication

The CrI3-WSe2 paper materials repeatedly center on:
- device geometry and heterostructure concept
- field sweeps with polarization-resolved PL
- spatial maps of polarization or splitting
- hysteresis and domain-dependent behavior
- supplementary extraction of peak position, intensity, linewidth, and polarization metrics

The gated CrI3 material centers on:
- bilayer magnetic states
- metamagnetic transition tuning
- gate-dependent RMCD or Kerr signals
- phase maps versus gate and field

Search heuristic:
- if you need to know how the lab typically constructs a “convincing” magnetism story, these figure families are the template
