# 2D Magnetism Field Map

Purpose:
- Compact working map of foundational 2D-magnetism papers and control-knob papers that are most relevant to Xu Lab’s optical and magneto-optical work.

## Chunk 1: Foundational discovery papers

1. Huang et al., "Layer-dependent ferromagnetism in a van der Waals crystal down to the monolayer limit" (Nature 2017)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/nature22391
- core result: establishes intrinsic ferromagnetism in atomically thin CrI3, with striking layer dependence
- why it matters here: this is one of the Xu-lab-defining discovery papers and the cleanest experimental anchor for optical detection of 2D magnetism
- practical use: cite when a current discussion needs the canonical origin point for CrI3-based 2D magnetism

2. Gong et al., "Discovery of intrinsic ferromagnetism in two-dimensional van der Waals crystals" (Nature 2017)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/nature22060
- core result: independent foundational observation of intrinsic 2D ferromagnetism in Cr2Ge2Te6
- why it matters here: broadens the concept from one special material to a material class
- practical use: use when a request needs the broader field starting point, not just the CrI3 branch

## Chunk 2: Control-knob papers that matter for current lab instincts

3. Huang et al., "Electrical control of 2D magnetism in bilayer CrI3" (Nature Nanotechnology 2018)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-018-0121-3
- core result: electrostatic control of bilayer CrI3 magnetism and metamagnetic switching
- why it matters here: direct ancestor of the lab’s continuing interest in gate-tuned magnetic phases and magneto-optical readout
- practical use: use when a current question involves field/gate control of hysteresis, AFM-FM switching, or magnetoelectric behavior

4. Seyler et al., "Valley Manipulation by Optically Tuning the Magnetic Proximity Effect in WSe2/CrI3 Heterostructures" (Nano Letters 2018)
- link: https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.nanolett.8b01105
- core result: optical tuning of magnetic proximity in WSe2/CrI3 heterostructures, with large exchange-field-like valley control
- why it matters here: this is the bridge between magnetism and optical valley physics, and it strongly overlaps with the lab’s current mode of reasoning
- practical use: use when interpreting polarization, splitting, or magnetic-proximity language in optical heterostructure measurements

5. Li et al., "Pressure-controlled interlayer magnetism in atomically thin CrI3" (Nature Materials 2019)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-019-0506-1
- core result: stacking/order-sensitive interlayer magnetism in CrI3 can be tuned with pressure, revealing strong coupling between magnetic state and structure
- why it matters here: useful whenever current data suggests that structure, stacking, or moiré registry may be controlling magnetic order
- practical use: use when a user is implicitly asking whether a magnetic anomaly could really be structural/stacking-sensitive

## Chunk 3: Optical and spectroscopic depth papers

6. Cenker et al., "Direct observation of two-dimensional magnons in atomically thin CrI3" (Nature Physics 2021)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-020-0999-1
- core result: magneto-Raman access to magnons and layer-/state-dependent magnetic excitations in CrI3
- why it matters here: shows that optical spectroscopy can reach beyond order-parameter readout into magnetic excitations
- practical use: use when current work edges toward Raman or excitation-spectrum interpretation rather than static MOKE/RMCD only

7. Zhang et al., "All-optical switching of magnetization in atomically thin CrI3" (Nature Materials 2022)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-022-01354-7
- core result: optical control of magnetization in atomically thin CrI3
- why it matters here: strengthens the principle that optics is not only a passive probe but can also be an active control knob for 2D magnetic order
- practical use: use when thinking about pump-induced changes, light-power dependence, or optical manipulation language

## Chunk 4: Broader magnetic-material context

8. Fei et al., "Two-dimensional itinerant ferromagnetism in atomically thin Fe3GeTe2" (Nature Materials 2018)
- link: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-018-0149-7
- core result: 2D itinerant ferromagnetism in Fe3GeTe2, extending the field beyond insulating CrI3-like magnets
- why it matters here: reminds the assistant that “2D magnetism” in this lab is not restricted to one mechanism or one material class
- practical use: use when the conversation requires broader 2D-magnet vocabulary or comparison beyond CrI3

## Chunk 5: Working interpretation map

If the current request is about:
- proving 2D magnetic order optically:
  - start with Huang 2017
- gate-controlled magnetic phase changes:
  - use Huang 2018
- optical proximity / valley control:
  - use Seyler 2018
- structural or stacking sensitivity:
  - use Li 2019
- magnetic excitations in optical spectra:
  - use Cenker 2021
- optical control rather than passive probing:
  - use Zhang 2022

## Chunk 6: Lab-specific takeaway

The literature most relevant to this lab says that 2D magnetism is not a single measurement mode.
It is a coupled space of:
- magnetic state
- stacking/structure
- gate field
- optical polarization and spectroscopy
- hysteresis and domain behavior

That is why RMCD, MOKE, PL, Raman, and transport are repeatedly used together rather than as separate subfields.
