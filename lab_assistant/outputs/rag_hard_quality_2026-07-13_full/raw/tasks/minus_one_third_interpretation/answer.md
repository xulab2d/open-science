**Inference**

For a `nu=-1/3` optical FQAH signature, competing explanations to consider are:

1. True fractional Chern/FQAH order: should show an incompressible, time-reversal-breaking state with filling-locked optical anomaly and, ideally, independent Hall/Streda-consistent evidence.
2. Ferromagnetic or valley-polarized but topologically trivial order: could produce RMCD/MOKE/PL circular dichroism without fractional Hall response.
3. Competing AFM or mixed magnetic state: especially if the signal is weak, domain-like, hysteretic, or strongly displacement-field dependent.
4. Exciton/trion artifact: moire-trapped excitons or density-dependent trion resonances can shift PL/MCD features without proving a fractional electronic gap.
5. Disorder/percolation/domain effects: optical averaging could see local magnetic/filling features even when transport is not globally quantized.

Evidence that would separate them: coincident optical anomaly, incompressibility, and fractional Hall/Streda response at the same filling; reproducible displacement-field and magnetic-field evolution; hysteresis consistent with TR-breaking but not merely optical heating or domains; spatial maps showing uniform filling-locked behavior rather than puddles; and control of exciton/trion resonance shifts away from the claimed many-body gap.
