**Inference**

Ranked hierarchy for claiming an FCI:

1. **Transport**: strongest first-pass evidence if it shows fractional topological response at the relevant filling. It rules out a simple trivial insulator or metal, but by itself does not rule out contact artifacts, percolation, edge-dominated paths, or spatial inhomogeneity.

2. **Compressibility / thermodynamics**: strongest bulk corroboration when it shows an incompressible state at the same filling. It rules out a purely transport-defined anomaly and supports a real bulk gap, especially when paired with magneto-optics (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence...`). It does not by itself prove topological order.

3. **Local probe**: spatially decisive evidence. It tests whether the bulk and edge structure match an FCI picture and rules out interpretations based only on global averaging, local disorder, edge reconstruction, or disconnected conducting paths (`paper:local_probe...`; `claim:local_probe...`).

4. **Optical / RMCD / PL**: useful state identifier and symmetry/magnetism probe. Filling-sensitive PL/RMCD rules out a featureless density-driven optical shift and supports association with integer/fractional Chern states (`claim:signatures...`). Alone, it is not enough to establish quantized topology.

5. **Thermodynamics as a broad category**: valuable when independent of transport and tied to the same filling; it rules out purely electrical artifacts and helps establish a bulk phase. In this context it overlaps strongly with compressibility and magneto-optic bulk evidence (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence...`).
