**Observation**

Displacement field acts as a layer potential control knob. In angle-aligned `MoTe2/WSe2` at `nu=1`, increasing field reportedly drives consecutive phases: frustrated Mott insulator → ferromagnetic QAH-Mott insulator → antiferromagnetic valley-coherent Mott state; the first transition is reported without observed charge-gap closure (`claim:promoted_2602_mote2_wse2_consecutive_tpts`).  

Related twisted-TMD theory says an applied layer potential difference can tune band topology, Mott gaps, spin-orbit terms, and effective Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interactions (`claim:band_topology_hubbard_model_heisenberg_model_and_dzyaloshinskii_moriya_interaction_in_twisted_bi`; `paper:band_topology_hubbard_model_heisenberg_model_and_dzyaloshinskii_moriya_interacti`).  

Relevant observables present in the context are compressibility and RMCD for the MoTe2/WSe2 transition report (`edge:promoted_2602_tpts_measured_by_compressibility`, `edge:promoted_2602_tpts_measured_by_rmcd`, listed in context exclusions), plus magnetotransport signatures such as field-driven Chern behavior and large out-of-plane negative magnetoresistance in twisted MoTe2 higher bands (`paper:interplay_between_topology_and_correlations_in_the_second_moire_band_of_twisted`).

**Inference**

Mechanistically, displacement field likely changes the layer polarization and hybridization of moire bands, thereby changing Berry curvature/Chern character and the balance among Mott localization, ferromagnetic spin-valley polarization, antiferromagnetic exchange, and valley coherence. That is consistent with both the observed MoTe2/WSe2 phase sequence and the theory link between layer potential, topology, Mott gaps, SOC, and DM interactions.

Observable discrimination:

- QAH regime: Chern/Hall transport response plus ferromagnetic RMCD/spin-valley polarization; expected to be insulating in compressibility if it is a QAH-Mott state (`claim:promoted_2602_mote2_wse2_consecutive_tpts`).
- AFM valley-coherent regime: insulating compressibility but reduced or sign-cancelling net RMCD compared with ferromagnetic QAH; valley coherence should require an optical/valley-sensitive probe beyond ordinary charge transport.
- Frustrated or trivial Mott regime: insulating compressibility without QAH Hall/Chern response; RMCD may be weak, absent, or non-ferromagnetic depending on the magnetic order.
- Field-driven topological transitions: changes in Hall/Chern response and RMCD order parameter are the cleanest topology/magnetism indicators; compressibility tests whether the transition involves charge-gap closure.

**Uncertainty**

The supplied context does not give numeric displacement fields, Hall resistance values, RMCD amplitudes, optical selection rules for valley coherence, or a full phase diagram. It also does not provide a direct twisted-MoTe2 displacement-field phase sequence analogous to the MoTe2/WSe2 `nu=1` claim, so the twisted-MoTe2 discussion is an inference from related topology/correlation observables and field-tunable TMD theory rather than a fully sourced claim.
