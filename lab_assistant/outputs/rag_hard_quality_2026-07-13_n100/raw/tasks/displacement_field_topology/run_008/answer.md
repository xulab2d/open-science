**Inference**

Displacement field tunes topology mainly by changing layer polarization/hybridization and therefore the effective moire miniband topology and interaction-derived magnetic terms. In MoTe2/WSe2 this can move the same integer-filled Mott system between topologically distinct correlated insulators without requiring a simple single-particle gap-closing picture, since the first reported transition lacks an observed charge-gap closure [claim:promoted_2602_mote2_wse2_consecutive_tpts].

Observable separation:

- QAH ferromagnetic Mott: insulating compressibility gap plus ferromagnetic RMCD signal and anomalous/Chern Hall response expected from “QAH” label [claim:promoted_2602_mote2_wse2_consecutive_tpts].
- AFM valley-coherent Mott: insulating state, suppressed or compensated net RMCD compared with ferromagnetic QAH, and signatures tied to valley coherence rather than net Chern ferromagnetism [claim:promoted_2602_mote2_wse2_consecutive_tpts].
- Frustrated/trivial Mott: insulating compressibility response but no QAH Hall signature and no clear ferromagnetic RMCD order parameter [claim:promoted_2602_mote2_wse2_consecutive_tpts].
- Valley-coherent vs trivial: both may be insulating and non-QAH; distinction requires a valley-coherence-sensitive probe, not just charge gap/compressibility.

It also does not specify whether “trivial” refers to the frustrated Mott phase or a separate band-trivial insulator, so that distinction remains underdetermined from this context alone.
