A related promoted claim says zero-field optical switching of integer and fractional Chern ferromagnets enables magnetic bistate cycling and spatially resolved domain-wall writing in twisted MoTe2 (`claim:promoted_2508_optical_switching_domain_walls`).

**Inference**

The local net distinguishes two roles for light. In the passive role, PL/RMCD/magneto-optics diagnose correlated topology by tracking filling-, magnetization-, or bulk-state-dependent optical signatures. In the active role, optical pumping changes the Chern-ferromagnetic state itself: it can switch states, cycle bistability, and write domain-wall textures. Thus the optical program appears to be moving from “read out Chern order” toward “write and manipulate Chern order.”

**Uncertainty**

The supplied context does not give detailed mechanisms, pump conditions, quantitative switching thresholds, device geometries, or whether the same optical-control protocol applies identically to integer and fractional states. The optical-control claims are marked medium confidence and partly derive from 2025 arXiv-linked/paper-shelf entries, so the net supports the distinction but not a full mechanistic or quantitative comparison.
