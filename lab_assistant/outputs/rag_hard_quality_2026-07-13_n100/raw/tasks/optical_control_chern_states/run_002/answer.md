**Inference**

The local net distinguishes two optical roles. In the passive mode, PL/RMCD/magneto-optics are diagnostic observables: they report filling, magnetization, or bulk/edge/topological state information without being framed as the state-preparation knob. In the active mode, optical pumping is treated as a control parameter that can manipulate the correlated Chern state itself, including switching ferromagnetic Chern states and writing domain walls.

For a PI-level takeaway: optical measurements have moved from “seeing” integer and fractional Chern states to “steering” them. The passive probe logic supports phase identification; the active-control logic suggests optical helicity/drive can become a knob for nonvolatile or spatially patterned topological ferromagnetism.
