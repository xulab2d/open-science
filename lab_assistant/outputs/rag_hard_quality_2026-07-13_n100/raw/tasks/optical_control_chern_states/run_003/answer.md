**Observation**

Passive optical probes: The knowledge net says trion PL and RMCD are filling-sensitive optical probes of integer and fractional Chern states in twisted MoTe2, with reported signatures near `nu = -1, -2/3, -3/5` (`claim:signatures_of_fractional_quantum_anomalous_hall_states_in_twisted_mote2_bilayer_trion_pl_and_rmc`). It also notes that local compressibility combined with magneto-optics gives bulk evidence for integer and fractional Chern states beyond transport (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence_of_fractional_chern_insulator_in_moire_mote2_local_compressibility_plus_m`).

Active optical control: A separate claim says optical pumping is now being used as an active control knob for integer and fractional Chern states, not only as readout (`claim:optical_control_of_integer_and_fractional_chern_insulators_optical_pumping_is_now_being_used_as`; `paper:optical_control_of_integer_and_fractional_chern_insulators`). Another curated claim reports zero-field optical switching of integer and fractional Chern ferromagnets, magnetic bistate cycling, and spatial writing of ferromagnetic domain walls in twisted MoTe2 (`claim:promoted_2508_optical_switching_domain_walls`).

**Inference**

The net separates two optical roles. PL/RMCD/magneto-optics are evidence channels when they report filling, magnetization, or bulk-state information without implying the light is changing the phase. Optical pumping/switching is a manipulation channel when the optical drive changes or selects the Chern ferromagnetic state, cycles bistability, or writes domain structures. The active-control claims therefore extend the optical story from “detect integer/fractional Chern states” to “manipulate integer/fractional Chern ferromagnets.”

**Uncertainty**

The supplied context does not include quantitative pump powers, switching fidelities, timescales, microscopic mechanism, or detailed distinction between integer and fractional responses. The active-control claims are marked medium confidence and partly sourced from arXiv/paper-shelf summaries, so the net supports the qualitative distinction but not detailed performance claims.
