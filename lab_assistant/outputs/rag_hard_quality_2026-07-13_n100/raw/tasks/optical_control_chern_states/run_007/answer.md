**Inference**

The knowledge net distinguishes two roles for light. In the passive-probe role, PL/RMCD/magneto-optics diagnose filling, magnetization, or bulk topological phases without being described as intentionally changing the state. In the active-control role, optical pumping is treated as a manipulation channel: the drive can switch or write Chern-ferromagnetic configurations, including integer and fractional regimes.

For a PI-level takeaway: optical readout has moved from “optics as evidence for Chern states” toward “optics as a knob for preparing, switching, and spatially patterning Chern ferromagnets.” The graph frames this as especially relevant for twisted MoTe2 and for linking PL helicity/optical drive to correlated topology (`claim:optical_control_of_integer_and_fractional_chern_insulators_optical_pumping_is_now_being_used_as`).

**Uncertainty**

The supplied context does not give microscopic mechanisms, pump wavelengths, powers beyond “low pump power,” switching times, device conditions, or quantitative fidelity. It also does not provide enough detail to judge whether the same optical-control mechanism applies identically to integer and fractional Chern states, only that both are claimed to be optically switchable/control-relevant.
