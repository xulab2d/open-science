**Observation**

1. **Transport**: Highest direct evidence for an FCI/FQAH assignment because it measures the global topological charge response. But transport alone can be contaminated by contacts, edge conduction, percolation, or inhomogeneous current paths. Context flags the need for “more than transport” in FCI arguments (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence_of_fractional_chern_insulator_in_moire_mote2_local_compressibility_plus_m`; `lab_assistant/knowledge/papers/transport_and_thermodynamics.md`).

2. **Compressibility**: Strong bulk evidence when it shows incompressibility at the fractional filling. It rules out a simple metal or continuously compressible charge puddle state, but by itself does not prove Chern topology (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence_of_fractional_chern_insulator_in_moire_mote2_local_compressibility_plus_m`).

3. **Local probe**: Strong cross-check because it can resolve bulk and edge structure instead of relying only on global transport. It rules out the interpretation that the signal is only a nonlocal transport artifact or uncontrolled spatial inhomogeneity (`paper:local_probe_of_bulk_and_edge_states_in_a_fractional_chern_insulator`; `claim:local_probe_of_bulk_and_edge_states_in_a_fractional_chern_insulator_local_probes_can_resolve_bot`; `lab_assistant/knowledge/papers/xu_group_recent_moire_states.md`).

4. **Optical / RMCD / PL**: Useful filling-sensitive evidence for correlated Chern states. Trion PL and RMCD show signatures near fractional and integer Chern fillings in twisted MoTe2, so they help rule out a featureless or unrelated insulating state and identify magnetic/valley-correlated order. They do not alone establish quantized topological transport (`claim:signatures_of_fractional_quantum_anomalous_hall_states_in_twisted_mote2_bilayer_trion_pl_and_rmc`).

5. **Thermodynamics**: Best used as bulk corroboration, especially when combined with local compressibility and magneto-optics. It rules out a purely transport-defined artifact by showing the phase has a bulk thermodynamic signature (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence_of_fractional_chern_insulator_in_moire_mote2_local_compressibility_plus_m`; `lab_assistant/knowledge/papers/transport_and_thermodynamics.md`).

**Inference**

A practical hierarchy is: **transport first for topology, compressibility/thermodynamics for bulk gap, local probe for spatial origin, optical/RMCD/PL for order-parameter and filling assignment**. The strongest FCI case is therefore not any single probe, but concordance: quantized anomalous Hall-like transport plus bulk incompressibility/thermodynamics plus local evidence that the bulk and edge structure are real, with optical signatures tying the state to magnetic/valley Chern physics.
