**Inference**

A practical evidence hierarchy for claiming an FCI is:

1. **Quantized transport**: strongest single diagnostic of topological response. It rules out a merely insulating charge gap, but by itself does **not** rule out percolation, contact artifacts, edge-dominated conduction, or spatial phase coexistence.

2. **Compressibility / thermodynamic gap**: establishes that the relevant filling is incompressible in the bulk. It rules out a purely transport-defined anomaly with no bulk charge gap.

3. **Local probe of bulk and edge**: tests spatial uniformity and edge/bulk correspondence. It rules out interpretations where the signal comes only from edges, disorder puddles, or inhomogeneous domains.

4. **Optical RMCD / PL**: useful as a filling-sensitive and magnetization/valley-sensitive proxy. It rules out some trivial “nothing changes at the fractional filling” interpretations, but is indirect: optical contrast alone does not prove fractional topology.

5. **Broader thermodynamics combined with optical/compressibility**: most persuasive as corroboration that the state is a bulk phase, not just a transport feature. It rules out accidental transport plateaus without a corresponding bulk energetic signature.
