**Observation**

1. **Transport**: strongest direct topological evidence when it shows fractional anomalous/Hall or edge conductance behavior; context cites transport evidence for fractional QSH at `nu=3` with edge conductance and zero anomalous Hall response (`claim:promoted_2402_fqsh_nu3_edge_conductance`). It rules out a simple trivial insulator or featureless metal, but by itself does not rule out contact/edge/percolation or spatial-inhomogeneity interpretations.

2. **Thermodynamics / compressibility**: bulk evidence that the state is incompressible at the relevant filling. The context explicitly says local compressibility plus magneto-optics gives **bulk evidence** for integer and fractional Chern states (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence_of_fractional_chern_insulator_in_moire_mote2_local_compressibility_plus_m`; `lab_assistant/knowledge/papers/transport_and_thermodynamics.md`). This rules out a purely transport-only artifact and helps distinguish a genuine bulk gap from edge/contact-dominated signals.

3. **Local probe**: spatially resolved confirmation of bulk and edge structure. The context says local probes can resolve both bulk and edge states rather than relying only on global transport (`paper:local_probe_of_bulk_and_edge_states_in_a_fractional_chern_insulator`; `claim:local_probe_of_bulk_and_edge_states_in_a_fractional_chern_insulator_local_probes_can_resolve_bot`). This rules out interpretations based on uncontrolled spatial inhomogeneity, edge reconstruction alone, or sample-averaged ambiguity.

4. **Optical / RMCD / PL**: filling-sensitive optical evidence. Trion PL and RMCD are described as probes of integer and fractional Chern states, with signatures near `nu = -1, -2/3, -3/5` (`claim:signatures_of_fractional_quantum_anomalous_hall_states_in_twisted_mote2_bilayer_trion_pl_and_rmc`). These rule out a phase-blind filling assignment and support magnetic/valley-correlated order, but are not by themselves proof of quantized topology.

**Inference**

A PI-level evidence hierarchy would be: **transport + bulk thermodynamic/compressibility gap together at the top**, then **local spatial probes**, then **optical/RMCD/PL as strong correlative diagnostics**. Transport establishes the topological response; compressibility/thermodynamics establish that the relevant filling is a bulk incompressible phase; local probes test whether the bulk/edge picture is spatially real; optical probes help assign filling, magnetism, and valley/correlation signatures.

**Uncertainty**

The supplied context does not give detailed numeric Hall quantization values, activation gaps, entropy/magnetization data, or a full thermodynamic protocol. So the ranking above is a synthesis from the provided claims, not a claim that any single cited experiment fully satisfies every criterion for an FCI.
