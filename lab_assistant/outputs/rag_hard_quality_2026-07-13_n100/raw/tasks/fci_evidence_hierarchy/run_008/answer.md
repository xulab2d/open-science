**Observation**

1. **Thermodynamics / local compressibility + magneto-optics**: strongest bulk evidence in supplied context. It is described as “bulk evidence for integer and fractional Chern states” and useful for arguments needing more than transport (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence_of_fractional_chern_insulator_in_moire_mote2_local_compressibility_plus_m`; `lab_assistant/knowledge/papers/transport_and_thermodynamics.md`).  
   Rules out: a purely edge/contact/transport artifact, because the evidence is bulk-sensitive.

2. **Local probe of bulk and edge states**: strong spatial evidence. Local probes can resolve both bulk and edge structure “rather than relying only on global transport” (`paper:local_probe_of_bulk_and_edge_states_in_a_fractional_chern_insulator`; `claim:local_probe_of_bulk_and_edge_states_in_a_fractional_chern_insulator_local_probes_can_resolve_bot`).  
   Rules out: spatial inhomogeneity, edge-only interpretations, or ambiguity about whether the signal is from bulk versus edge.

3. **Transport**: necessary but not sufficient. The context explicitly contrasts stronger probes against “global transport,” implying transport alone is vulnerable to non-bulk interpretations (`claim:local_probe_of_bulk_and_edge_states_in_a_fractional_chern_insulator_local_probes_can_resolve_bot`). A related transport claim notes fractional QSH evidence via edge conductance and zero anomalous Hall response (`claim:promoted_2402_fqsh_nu3_edge_conductance`).  
   Rules out: some trivial metallic/insulating scenarios and, when quantized/anomalous Hall-like, supports topology; does not by itself rule out edge/contact/percolation artifacts.

4. **Compressibility alone**: bulk-sensitive evidence for incompressible/gapped states, but in the supplied context it appears strongest when paired with magneto-optics (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence_of_fractional_chern_insulator_in_moire_mote2_local_compressibility_plus_m`).  
   Rules out: a simple compressible metal; does not alone establish Chern character from the supplied context.

5. **Optical / RMCD / PL**: useful filling-sensitive and magnetic/topological proxy evidence. Trion PL and RMCD can probe integer and fractional Chern states in twisted MoTe2, with signatures near listed fillings (`claim:signatures_of_fractional_quantum_anomalous_hall_states_in_twisted_mote2_bilayer_trion_pl_and_rmc`). Optical pumping can also control integer/fractional Chern states (`claim:optical_control_of_integer_and_fractional_chern_insulators_optical_pumping_is_now_being_used_as`).  
   Rules out: absence of filling-correlated optical/magnetic response; does not alone rule out all non-topological correlated or magnetic states.

**Inference**

A conservative hierarchy for identifying a fractional Chern insulator is:

**thermodynamics/local compressibility + magneto-optics > local probe > transport > compressibility alone > optical/RMCD/PL alone.**

The most convincing case is multi-probe convergence: transport gives the topological response, compressibility/thermodynamics establish a bulk gap or incompressibility, local probes show the spatial origin of bulk and edge structure, and RMCD/PL link the state to filling-dependent magnetic/valley/topological order. Each probe removes a different loophole; none of the supplied context says a single optical or transport measurement is definitive by itself.

**Uncertainty**

The supplied context does not give quantitative benchmarks, exact Hall values, gap sizes, entropy/magnetization criteria, or a formal community-standard ranking. The ranking above is therefore an evidence-hierarchy inference from the provided graph claims, especially the repeated emphasis that bulk thermodynamic/local evidence is needed beyond global transport.
