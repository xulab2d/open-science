**Inference**

For a reported optical `nu=-1/3` FQAH signature, competing interpretations/artifacts to consider are:

1. **Ordinary valley/spin polarization or ferromagnetism without fractional Hall topology.**  
   RMCD/MOKE contrast can indicate time-reversal breaking, but not by itself fractional Hall conductance. Separation requires a quantized or Streda-consistent response tied to the filling, ideally with transport Hall data or bulk compressibility plus magneto-optics (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence_of_fractional_chern_insulator_in_moire_mote2_compressibility_plus_magneto`).

2. **Moiré exciton/trion spectral shifts unrelated to an electronic FQAH ground state.**  
   Moiré potentials can trap excitons and produce strong valley-selective optical signatures (`claim:signatures_of_moire_trapped_valley_excitons_in_mose2_wse2_heterobilayers_interlayer_excitons_can`). Separation requires showing the optical anomaly tracks electronic filling and incompressibility, not just exciton resonance structure, disorder-localized emitters, or optical selection-rule changes.

3. **Competing magnetic/correlated phases, including AFM or non-topological insulating states.**  
   The context notes competing FCI and antiferromagnetic ground states in moire MoTe2, with electric-field sensitivity (`paper:evidence_of_competing_ground_states_between_fractional_chern_insulator_and_antif`). Separation requires displacement-field, magnetic-field, and density dependence showing behavior expected for a fractional Chern state rather than an AFM or trivial correlated insulator.

4. **Device/measurement artifacts.**  
   Likely artifacts include hysteresis from charge traps, optical heating, strain/disorder inhomogeneity, gate cross-talk, or reflectance/PL background changes masquerading as circular dichroism. These are not specifically documented in the supplied context, so this is a generic experimental-control inference rather than a sourced claim.

**Uncertainty**

The context is insufficient to state what was actually observed at `nu=-1/3`, which optical channel was used, or whether the signal included quantized Hall, Streda slope, compressibility, or reproducibility across devices. The decisive evidence would be co-registered optical contrast, incompressibility, and Hall/Streda-consistent fractional Chern response at exactly `nu=-1/3`; without those, an optical topological interpretation should remain provisional.
