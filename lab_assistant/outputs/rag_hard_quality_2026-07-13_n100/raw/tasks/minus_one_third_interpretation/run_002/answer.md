**Inference**

For a `nu=-1/3` optical FQAH assignment, competing interpretations/artifacts to consider are:

1. **Ferromagnetic or valley-polarized correlated insulator without proven fractional Hall topology.**  
   RMCD/MOKE/MCD can indicate time-reversal breaking, but topology would need agreement with a fractional Streda slope, quantized Hall response, or another bulk/topological diagnostic (`claim:optical_probes_primary_evidence`; `claim:promoted_2304_fqah_streda_optical_mcd`).

2. **Competing magnetic ground state, including antiferromagnetism or field-tuned phase competition.**  
   The context explicitly flags AFM–FCI competition in intermediate-angle MoTe2 and electric-field switching between regimes (`paper:evidence_of_competing_ground_states_between_fractional_chern_insulator_and_antif`). Separation would require showing that the `nu=-1/3` optical feature tracks the FQAH phase boundary rather than an AFM/ferromagnetic transition.

3. **Excitonic or moiré-trapping optical structure masquerading as an electronic topological signature.**  
   Moiré-trapped interlayer excitons can produce strong valley-selective optical signatures independent of fractional Hall topology (`claim:signatures_of_moire_trapped_valley_excitons_in_mose2_wse2_heterobilayers_interlayer_excitons_can`). Evidence separating this would be density/filling-locked behavior at `nu=-1/3`, hysteresis/TR-breaking tied to the electronic state, and consistency with transport or compressibility.

4. **Local optical sensitivity to domains, disorder, or probe-dependent selection rules.**  
   This is an inference from the stated need to treat optics as state-sensitive but not automatically definitive (`claim:optical_probes_primary_evidence`). Spatially resolved optical maps, reproducibility across spots/devices, and correlation with bulk incompressibility or Hall response would help separate intrinsic FQAH from local artifacts.
