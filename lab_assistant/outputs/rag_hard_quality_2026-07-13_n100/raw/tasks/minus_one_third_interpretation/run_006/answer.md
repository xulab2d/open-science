**Observation**

Optical probes can be state-sensitive primary evidence in moire MoTe2, including PL, RMCD, MOKE, and pump-probe, but they are not automatically equivalent to transport quantization (`claim:optical_probes_primary_evidence`). Moire excitons can be trapped by the moire potential and carry valley-selective optical signatures, so optical contrast can reflect excitonic localization/selection rules as well as electronic topology (`claim:signatures_of_moire_trapped_valley_excitons_in_mose2_wse2_heterobilayers_interlayer_excitons_can`). Existing cited FQAH optical evidence in the supplied context is for MCD/trion-PL Streda-like behavior at `nu=-2/3` and `-3/5`, not explicitly `nu=-1/3` (`claim:promoted_2304_fqah_streda_optical_mcd`). Transport Hall plateaus are the strongest direct FQAH/IQAH evidence in twisted MoTe2 (`claim:observation_of_fractionally_quantized_anomalous_hall_effect_direct_hall_plateau_evidence_for_iqa`).

**Inference**

For a reported `nu=-1/3` optical FQAH signature, competing interpretations/artifacts to consider are:

1. **Valley-polarized or ferromagnetic correlated state without proven fractional Hall response.**  
   Optical circular dichroism or PL polarization can indicate time-reversal breaking, but separating “magnetic fractional Chern insulator” from ordinary valley/spin polarization requires Hall conductance, Streda slope, or bulk incompressibility evidence.

2. **Exciton/trion spectroscopy artifact.**  
   Because moire excitons can be trapped and valley-selective, a PL/RMCD feature near `nu=-1/3` could arise from exciton energy shifts, oscillator-strength changes, local charge environment, or trion formation rather than a topological ground state (`claim:signatures_of_moire_trapped_valley_excitons_in_mose2_wse2_heterobilayers_interlayer_exciton...`).

3. **Competing magnetic order, including antiferromagnetism.**  
   The context notes competition between FCI and antiferromagnetic ground states in moire MoTe2, especially with electric-field sensitivity (`paper:evidence_of_competing_ground_states_between_fractional_chern_insulator_and_antif`). A weak or field-sensitive optical magnetic signal could therefore be non-FQAH magnetism.

4. **Device inhomogeneity or nearby-filling contamination.**  
   Optical probes average over illuminated regions and can be sensitive to domains. A feature assigned to `nu=-1/3` could reflect puddles/domains at neighboring incompressible or magnetic fillings unless spatial, density, and displacement-field dependence are controlled.

Evidence that would separate these:

- **Quantized Hall response at the same filling and conditions** would be the cleanest discriminator, because direct Hall plateaus are identified as strongest direct evidence (`claim:observation_of_fractionally_quantized_anomalous_hall_effect_direct_hall_plateau_evidence_for_iqa`).
- **Streda-consistent density shift of the optical feature with magnetic field** would support fractional Chern character, analogous to the supplied `nu=-2/3` and `-3/5` optical MCD/trion-PL evidence (`claim:promoted_2304_fqah_streda_optical_mcd`).
- **Bulk incompressibility coincident with the optical feature** would help distinguish a true insulating fractional state from excitonic spectral contrast (`claim:thermodynamic_evidence_of_fractional_chern_insulator_in_moire_mote2_compressibility_plus_magneto`).
- **Hysteresis/remanence plus domain imaging tied to the same filling** would support time-reversal breaking, but still needs transport/Streda/compressibility to establish FQAH rather than generic magnetism.
- **Systematic displacement-field, temperature, excitation-power, and spatial mapping controls** would test AFM competition, heating/photo-doping, exciton artifacts, and inhomogeneity.

**Uncertainty**

The supplied context does not include the excluded `nu=-1/3` optical-specific node content, raw spectra, device parameters, or any claimed numeric optical/Streda slopes. Therefore I cannot say that `nu=-1/3` optical data demonstrate FQAH. From the provided evidence, the cautious conclusion is: optical signatures at `nu=-1/3` are plausible evidence for a correlated valley/magnetic state, but FQAH assignment requires coincident quantized transport or Streda/compressibility evidence that rules out excitonic, magnetic-competing-order, and inhomogeneity artifacts.
